## @tylavr

Participating in the Environmental, Social, and Governance reporting course with The ESG Institute has taught me not only about sustainable practices in businesses, but how we can apply these principles and frameworks required in real-world scenarios. Sustainability is invaluable in nature conservation, food security, and communities in educational and industry outlook. 
A relevant example in South Africa is the case for fracking of non-renewable resources in the Karoo, in discussion because of the supply cliff in Mozambique by 2028. This initiative was halted in South Africa by the Supreme Court of Appeal given safety and health violations in the Environmental Management Act (Act No. 107 of 1998). The Global Reporting Initiative (GRI) standards require accurate disclosures to be incorporated in these litigation cases. This may include collecting data on the planned permits and radioactivity or chemicals materially impacting health, tourism, society, the economy, biodiversity or resources, such as in Strategic Environmental Assessment (SEA) reports or greenhouse emissions investigations in South Africa. 
The GRI and ESG pillars will have an important role in building trust, and protecting the voices and interests of the local communities and environment. 
This certificate has prepared me with a new perspective to responsible decision-making for a sustainable future in the era of digitalisation and data-driven transformation. How can organisations apply sustainability reporting to balance investor or regulator trust, with environmental and social responsibility?
